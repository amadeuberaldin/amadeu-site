# Site Identity

Owner: Amadeu Beraldin

This website represents the technical journey and experiments of Amadeu Beraldin.

It is not intended to look like a traditional developer portfolio or a marketing page.

Instead, it should feel like a public laboratory of ideas, systems and technical exploration.

The goal is to show:

- curiosity
- experimentation
- systems thinking
- technical independence
- learning through building

---

# Core Philosophy

Technology is best learned by building real systems.

Many experiments begin as small ideas and evolve into working infrastructure through iteration and documentation.

This site documents that process.

It should feel honest, calm, technical and thoughtful.

Avoid exaggerated claims or marketing language.

---

# Homepage Philosophy

The homepage should not be a list of projects.

It should feel like an introduction to a technical mindset.

Avoid:

- blog-style listing
- repetitive cards
- long lists of technologies
- marketing language

Prefer:

- strong headline
- short narrative
- visual clarity
- elegant layout
- philosophical tone

The homepage should feel like a **technical manifesto** rather than a project catalog.

---

# Topic Separation

Topics such as:

- homelab
- minecraft
- networking
- automation
- AI agents

should exist as **separate pages**.

The homepage should only hint at them through navigation.

---

# AI Usage Disclosure

It is acceptable to mention the use of AI agents in the development of the site, but the framing must be balanced.

Example tone:

"This site was developed with the support of an agent pipeline that generates, validates and publishes preview versions locally.  
The architecture, testing, review and final curation were conducted by Amadeu Beraldin."

This communicates automation while preserving technical ownership.

---

# Content Integrity Rules

The site must never:

- invent clients
- invent metrics
- invent companies
- invent achievements
- invent professional history

All content must remain grounded in real experiments and learning.

---

# Tone

The tone should be:

- reflective
- technical
- calm
- thoughtful
- precise

The site should feel like **engineering notes made public**, not marketing material.
